Project Summary

MegaloTorus logo. Credit: Sierra B. Estrella

What *IS* MegaloTorus?

MegaloTorus is a toroid-shaped immersive visual/audio "cinematic" experience.

1600 digitally addressable RGB LEDs are meticulously hand-sequenced to sets of curated music.*

*Can be auto-sequenced to input sources (including live music) but results are considerably less spectacular. Some expensive Windows-only software may be able to help this effort.

Project Summary

MegaloTorus logo. Credit: Sierra B. Estrella

What is the goal of MegaloTorus?

Create an immersive and relaxing environment that fuels the imagination, nurtures creativity, and fosters community.*

*Art as a place.

Physical Description

MegaloTorus structure model side view.

MegaloTorus structure model top view.

Completed structure without the cover.

Structure

  • 16 x 30 ft. concentric arches.
  • Arches are ~3/4 of a circle.
  • Outer circle: 16 evenly spaced anchors placed 14 ft. radially from center.
  • Inner circle: 16 evenly spaced anchors placed 2 ft. radially from center.
  • Each arch is rotated about the inner anchor in the x-y plane by three outer anchor positions counter-clockwise.
  • To maintain the 3/4 circle shape, anchor pairs are tilted away from each other at an angle of 75º from the x-y plane.

Physical Description

MegaloTorus cover schematics. Credit: George O'Donnell.

Sewing the cover together.

Cover

  • Designed by George O'Donnell and Daniel Tenenbaum
  • A templating and coordinate system were used to translate the designs onto six oz., 12 mil poly fabric.
  • Pieces were sewn together using a standard sewing maching.

Sewing the cover together.

Cover

Physical Description

LEDs

  • 1600 ws2811 LEDs spaced approximately 3" apart along the entire length of each of the structure's "spines".
  • Controlled by an AlphaPix Flex controller.
  • Modeled and sequenced with xLights.*

*This is our "canvas".

Physical Description

Interior

  • Chillax / cuddle space
  • Room for chairs, hammocks, dancing*
  • Used during the day for workshops, yoga, and other community events.

*It may look small in the photo but it is cavernous in real life - roomy enough for comfortably spinning poi. The center is monolithic and has been compared by patrons to a large tree or something from Avatar.

Why do we need an art grant?

Self funded: $4871.22

Inflation sucks 😖

We learned some hard lessons:

  • Structure + cover - wind sucks 😭
  • Efficiency - setup / teardown needs to be less demanding 😩

Art Grant

Structural improvements

Priority: High

Cost: $500.00

  • #11 rebar: we used #4 which isn't strong enough - it bends ☹️

Trial and error required:

  • Stability rings and/or inter-arch connectors
  • Other tie-down methods (eg. lag-bolts and straps?)

Art Grant

Efficiency improvements

Priority: High

Cost: $300.00

This Goliath is a labor of love. We are actively working to identify where we can gain efficiency in the setup / teardown process and remediate

  • 6 in. bungee balls for stringing the lights
  • Better tiedown method - sleeves to hold the guylines in place (like the sleeves the poles go through on a camping tent) making the guylines a more permanent part of the cover
  • Sewing together the center column
  • A larger trailer that can fit/accomodate all of the parts*

*Stretch goal. Cost not factored in, would definitely exceed the grant.

Art Grant

Cover improvements

Priority: Medium

Cost: $200.00

  • Air vents to keep the wind from pulling on it as much
  • Screen doors - hand-sized moths love this place! 👐❤️

Art Grant

Controller upgrade

Priority: Low

Cost: $150.00

Currently the controller has to be wired to a laptop computer to run sequences (very long wires). Sequences cannot be queued up - regular human intervention is required to switch up sequences.

A Falcon Pi Player will allow us to

  • queue sequences
  • Run sequences independent of an external laptop computer (no more ridiculously long wires!)
